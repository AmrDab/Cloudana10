What this is, plainly. A front-end prototype of Cloudana's Browser Verifier. The verification is real — your browser re-executes each certificate's block-matrix multiplication, rebuilds the transcript hash, recomputes the proof z, and confirms it meets difficulty (the same redundancy check the testnet orchestrator performs). In this demo the certificates are generated locally; in production they are drawn from the orchestrator's challenger queue.

Credits are testnet only. They carry no monetary value and are not a token. Nothing here is a promise that they ever convert to one — any future distribution is subject to legal review and is not committed. Connecting a wallet establishes identity; a separate signed message authorizes compute. Work runs only while this tab is open, throttled to your chosen intensity, and stops on demand. Desktop on power recommended; heavy use on battery drains it.
