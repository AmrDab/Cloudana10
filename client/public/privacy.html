Privacy Policy

What we collect, said plainly.

Effective 11 June 2026 · applies to cloudana.io, app.cloudana.io and api.cloudana.io

This site (cloudana.io)

The landing site is static. It sets no cookies, runs no analytics or tracking scripts, and stores nothing about you. Fonts load from Google Fonts, and the page makes one request to our own API (api.cloudana.io/health) to show live status — neither request includes anything about you beyond what any HTTP request carries.

The console and API

If you create an account or register a provider node, we process what the protocol needs to function:

  • Account data — the email or wallet address you sign in with.
  • Provider data — hardware specifications (CPU, GPU, RAM, disk) and a device fingerprint, used for tier classification and anti-Sybil protection. Registrations are recorded on the Base blockchain, which is public and permanent.
  • Payment data — handled by Stripe; we never see or store card numbers.
  • Operational logs — our infrastructure runs on Cloudflare, which processes IP addresses and request metadata to serve and protect the service.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell or rent your data. To anyone.
  • We don't run advertising or cross-site tracking.
  • We don't collect more than the protocol needs to work.

The blockchain caveat

Anything written on-chain — wallet addresses, provider registrations, certificate hashes, rewards — is public by design and cannot be deleted by us or anyone. Don't put anything in an on-chain transaction you wouldn't want to be permanent.

Your choices & contact

To access or delete the off-chain data tied to your account, or to ask anything about this policy, open an issue on GitHub — the project is built in the open and that's the fastest route to us.

Honesty note: this is a plain-language policy for a testnet-stage project, written to be true rather than exhaustive. Before mainnet it will be reviewed by counsel and versioned here.
